About Fredducation

The goal of Fredducation is to encourage language learners to interact with their target language in the real world. I aim to accomplish this by sharing my experiences as I learn Spanish, specifically with the people, places, media, food (yum!), and resources that I have learned so much from. I felt that the language learning community that I interacted with was overly concerned with things like counting the hours of listening comprehension exercises until a milestone was reached, debating which learning apps bring fluency (the answer is none), or how to attain a certain accent in a target language. I thought to myself “isn’t the point of it all to enjoy the language?”. So I made a space to show how I enjoy interacting with my target language, Spanish. Hopefully this pulls people away from what’s not important in their own language learning journey.

About Me

Why is it called Fredducation?

It’s not just a play on my name and word education. It’s also a recognition that all of the posts on this site are about the many teachers in my language education. I do not consider myself an autodidact. Through this site I'm trying to show gratitude for all the things that have taught me something. Originally it was going to be Freducation with only one d, but someone already owned most of the top-level domains for that name. Also I've always been more of a Freddy than a Fred, so Fredducation it was.

What's in a Logo?

I didn't think of making a logo for the site until the project was well under way. I'm not quite sure how it came to me but I thought something along the lines of "langauge learning is a lot like explicit functions in algebra". So we take some function, f, with input x, and outcome y, represented all together as f(x) = y and we're ready to do our homework. With respect to langauge learning and myself, I (Freddy) am the function, the input (independent variable) is the language, and the outcome (dependent variable) is my ability to comprehend that langauge. So we arrive at the logo, f(ñ), which is seen above this section and on the top left corner of the site's navbar. The ñ seemed like a perfect fit to represent Spanish. Maybe in the future I'll target another language and plug in a character from that writing system. As for how to represent the output of that function in the logo? I have no idea. Let's say the output is the site. It's purple like everything else because why not?
