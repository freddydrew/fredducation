About Fredducation

Initially I made Fredducation because I wanted to build web development and programming skills. I thought a programming project closely related to one of my interests, learning Spanish, wouldn't feel like a chore and might even be fun. I really enjoyed building it and still have fun working on it on my spare time but the site's purpose in my life has changed. Now I use it to reflect on how I learned more about something that is close to my heart and to show gratitude for these moments in my life. When I first brought the site to life I thought "now I have to write articles all the time", as if it was a bother to commit to something like this, but those thoughts went away. I realized how good writing an article made me feel. When I look at photos and piece together their story for a post, I feel a deep appreciation for life. Today, that's why I'm writing articles on Fredducation. I hope that other people read what I post and feel good too. The posts focus on the people, places, media, and resources that have taught me something, with an underlying theme of travel. I try to post once a week but I'm not too strict about it, mostly I post when it feels right.

About Me

I'm Freddy Drew, creator of Fredducation. I'm just a normal guy that enjoys riding his bike, traveling, catching a baseball game, and absolutely butchering his second language. Growing up I didn't appreciate language learning or education in general but sometime recently that changed. Now I'm learning, I'm loving it, and I'm grateful for the opportunities to do so. That's me in the photo below. On the day that photo was taken I learned that staying hydrated on a hike is important.

Why is it called Fredducation?

I wanted to convey that the posts on this site are small pieces of my or Freddy's education. When I considered my website name, that didn't role off the tongue very well, so the play on the two words was born. Originally it was going to be Freducation with only one d, but someone already owned most of the top-level domains for that name. I've always been more of a Freddy than a Fred anyways, so Fredducation it was.

What's in a Logo?

I didn't think of making a logo for the site until the project was well under way. I'm not quite sure how it came to me but I thought something along the lines of "langauge learning is a lot like explicit functions in algebra". So we take some function, f, with input x, and outcome y, represented all together as f(x) = y and we're ready to do our homework. With respect to langauge learning and myself, I (Freddy) am the function, the input (independent variable) is the language, and the outcome (dependent variable) is my ability to comprehend that langauge. So we arrive at the logo, f(ñ), which is seen above this section and on the top left corner of the site's navbar. The ñ seemed like a perfect fit to represent Spanish. Maybe in the future I'll target another language and plug in a character from that writing system. As for how to represent the output of that function in the logo? I have no idea. Let's say the output is the site and my poor grammar in real life.
